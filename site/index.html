Streaming lost something

“A grid of tiny cover thumbnails you scroll past and forget. No shelf, no albums, nothing to browse.”

The physical joy of flipping through a collection disappeared when the music went digital.

Crate gives it back

Your streaming library becomes a tactile, always-on object in the room. All the catalog of streaming, all the browse-ability of a record crate — on commodity hardware you can build yourself.

Everything on the wall

The Shelf
Your whole library as CD jewel-cases across an ultrawide display. Real or generated cover art, duration-scaled widths, per-album typography.

Shelves & playlists
Curated, reorderable collections, each with its own sort. Playlists render as song shelves you can view, reorder, and hide tracks in.

One box across your sources: On your shelf → In your library → From your sources, plus artists and songs. Explicit tags, recent searches.
Multi-room audio
Send playback to any player Music Assistant knows — Sonos, AirPlay, and more. Pick a speaker or group, save one-tap presets, per-room and proportional volume.
Honest playback feedback
A “connecting” spinner and frozen seek until the room actually reports playing — nothing animates before the audio starts.
Pinch to zoom
Pinch the shelf to change density or bring up a magnifier loupe. Browse a dense wall or lean into a single case.
Idle, attract & sleep
Idle and attract modes, a sleep schedule, and brightness/display control keep the wall looking right around the clock.
Companion admin app
A phone/desktop app to import & curate the library, build shelves, edit case art, manage speakers/presets, and configure everything.
Your streaming services
Plays whatever Music Assistant supports — Apple Music, Spotify, and more. Your existing library and subscriptions, on the wall.
Live system status
A health panel for the shelf, admin, server, and Music Assistant — uptime, connection counts, and a per-service restart.
Open & self-hosted
Open-source, commodity hardware, and your own Music Assistant instance. No cloud lock-in — it runs on your wall, on your network.

What is Crate?

Crate renders your streaming collection as physical CD jewel-cases on an ultrawide, wall-mounted touchscreen. Tap a case, the album flips open, hit play, and it streams to your speakers through Music Assistant. It's open-source and runs on commodity hardware.

The two apps

  • The shelf — the always-on kiosk on the wall. Touch and gesture driven, sized for an ultrawide display.
  • The admin — a phone/desktop companion for importing, curating, and configuring everything behind the wall.

Both are served by a single backend process that holds the connection to Music Assistant. See Architecture for the full picture.

Develop

npm install

# 1. Device service (REST + WS + SQLite + artwork) on :8080
MA_URL=http://<ma-host>:8095 MA_TOKEN=<token> npm run dev:server

# 2. Shelf (kiosk UI) on :5173
npm run dev:shelf

# 3. Admin (search + curate) on :5174
npm run dev:admin

Open the admin at http://localhost:5174, search for an album, and add it. It appears on the shelf at http://localhost:5173 — tap it and press play.

Architecture

One backend process, two browser front-ends it serves, and a single connection to Music Assistant.

Placeholder. See ARCHITECTURE.md in the repo for the current, authoritative version.

The pieces

  • server — the single Node process. HTTP API + /ws fan-out hub, SQLite, and one persistent WebSocket to Music Assistant.
  • shelf — the wall/kiosk Vite SPA, served at /.
  • admin — the phone/desktop Vite SPA, served at /admin/.

The front-ends are static bundles with no runtime of their own; the server mounts each dist/ at its route. Both share types and the API client via @crate/shared.

Why Music Assistant?

Phase 0 proved node-sonos-http-api can't reliably start arbitrary albums on a real Sonos household. Music Assistant streams to Sonos with the operator's real credentials and exposes search, metadata, artwork, playback, and live state over one authenticated WebSocket.
